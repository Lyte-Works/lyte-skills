# M&A Playbook

## When to Use
- Evaluating a potential acquisition
- Preparing the company to be acquired
- Due diligence on a target company
- Post-acquisition integration planning

## Workflow

### Step 1: Acquisition Criteria
Define what makes a good acquisition target:
| Criteria | Requirement |
|----------|------------|
| Strategic fit | Complements our product/market |
| Revenue | Minimum ARR threshold |
| Technology | Compatible or additive to our stack |
| Team | Key talent we want to retain |
| Customer overlap | Complementary, not identical |
| Culture | Compatible values and working style |

### Step 2: Target Evaluation
| Area | Assessment |
|------|-----------|
| **Product** | What does the product do? Is it complementary? |
| **Revenue** | ARR, growth rate, unit economics |
| **Customers** | Who are they? Overlap with ours? Churn rate? |
| **Technology** | Stack compatibility, technical debt, scalability |
| **Team** | Key people, culture, retention risk |
| **Legal** | IP ownership, contracts, liabilities |
| **Financial** | Profitability, cash position, obligations |

### Step 3: Due Diligence Checklist
**Financial:**
- [ ] 3 years of financial statements
- [ ] Revenue breakdown by customer/product
- [ ] MRR/ARR with churn analysis
- [ ] Cash flow and runway
- [ ] Outstanding debts or obligations

**Technical:**
- [ ] Architecture review
- [ ] Code quality assessment
- [ ] Security audit
- [ ] Technical debt assessment
- [ ] Infrastructure costs

**Legal:**
- [ ] IP ownership confirmed
- [ ] Customer contracts reviewed
- [ ] Employee agreements reviewed
- [ ] Pending or potential litigation
- [ ] Regulatory compliance status

**Team:**
- [ ] Key personnel identified
- [ ] Retention packages planned
- [ ] Culture assessment
- [ ] Reporting structure post-merger

### Step 4: Valuation Framework
Common SaaS valuation methods:
- **Revenue multiple**: ARR x multiple (ranges by growth rate and market)
- **DCF**: Discounted cash flow for profitable companies
- **Comparable transactions**: What similar companies sold for

### Step 5: Integration Plan
| Phase | Timeline | Activities |
|-------|----------|-----------|
| Day 1 | Announcement | Team communication, customer notification |
| Week 1-4 | Stabilize | Keep everything running, no changes |
| Month 2-3 | Integrate | Systems, processes, team integration |
| Month 4-6 | Optimize | Eliminate redundancies, realize synergies |
| Month 7-12 | Scale | Combined product roadmap, unified go-to-market |

## Quality Gate
- [ ] Acquisition criteria defined before evaluating targets
- [ ] Target evaluation covers all areas (product, revenue, tech, team, legal, financial)
- [ ] Due diligence checklist completed
- [ ] Valuation justified with methodology
- [ ] Integration plan covers first 12 months

## Anti-Patterns
- **Acquiring without criteria** — opportunistic acquisitions without strategic fit waste money.
- **Skipping due diligence** — enthusiasm is not a substitute for thorough evaluation.
- **No integration plan** — the acquisition is day one. Integration is the real work.
- **Ignoring culture** — incompatible cultures kill more acquisitions than bad products.
- **Rushing** — acquisition decisions made under time pressure often regret later.
