# CEO Advisor

## When to Use
- Strategic decision-making at the business level
- Setting company direction and priorities
- Evaluating market opportunities or pivots
- Leadership challenges (team, culture, growth)
- Preparing for board meetings or investor conversations

## Workflow

### Step 1: Strategic Assessment
- **Where are we now?** (revenue, team, product, market position)
- **Where do we want to be?** (12-month vision)
- **What is the biggest obstacle?** (the one thing blocking progress)
- **What are we avoiding?** (the hard decision not being made)

### Step 2: Priority Framework
A CEO should have 3 priorities maximum per quarter:
1. [Priority 1]: The most important thing
2. [Priority 2]: The second most important thing
3. [Priority 3]: The third most important thing

Everything else is a distraction. If everything is a priority, nothing is.

### Step 3: Decision Framework
For significant decisions:
1. **What is the decision?** (state it clearly)
2. **What are the options?** (at least 2, plus "do nothing")
3. **What are the trade-offs?** (what do you gain and lose with each option?)
4. **What is reversible?** (reversible decisions should be made quickly)
5. **What would you advise someone else to do?** (removes ego)
6. **What is the cost of waiting?** (sometimes delay is the biggest cost)

### Step 4: Leadership Coaching
Common CEO challenges:
| Challenge | Advice |
|-----------|--------|
| Can't let go of tasks | Delegate or automate. Your job is strategy and people, not execution. |
| Too many priorities | Pick 3. Say no to everything else this quarter. |
| Avoiding hard conversations | Have them this week. Delay makes them harder. |
| No time to think | Block 2 hours/week for strategic thinking. Protect it. |
| Decision fatigue | Create frameworks and policies so you make decisions once, not repeatedly. |

### Step 5: Board/Investor Communication
When preparing for stakeholder communication:
- **Lead with the story**: where we are, where we're going, how we'll get there
- **Be honest about challenges**: credibility comes from transparency
- **Show metrics**: revenue, growth, unit economics
- **Clear ask**: what do you need from the board? (advice, connections, capital)

## Anti-Patterns
- **Everything is urgent** — if everything is a priority, you have no priorities.
- **Avoiding decisions** — indecision is a decision (to accept the status quo).
- **Working IN the business, not ON it** — CEOs who do execution have no time for strategy.
- **Echo chamber** — only hearing what you want to hear leads to blind spots.
- **No documentation** — strategic decisions that live only in the CEO's head are not strategy.
