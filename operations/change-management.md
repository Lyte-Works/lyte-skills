# Change Management

## When to Use
- Implementing new tools, processes, or workflows
- Organizational restructuring
- Strategy shifts that affect the team
- Technology migrations
- Any change that requires people to work differently

## Workflow

### Step 1: Define the Change
| Element | Answer |
|---------|--------|
| What is changing? | Specific description |
| Why is it changing? | Business reason |
| Who is affected? | Teams, roles, individuals |
| What does success look like? | Measurable outcome |
| When does it happen? | Timeline |

### Step 2: Stakeholder Analysis
| Stakeholder | Impact Level | Current Stance | Needed Stance | Action |
|------------|-------------|---------------|--------------|--------|
| [Name/Group] | High/Med/Low | Supportive/Neutral/Resistant | Supportive | [Action] |

### Step 3: Communication Plan
| When | Who | Message | Channel |
|------|-----|---------|---------|
| Announcement | All affected | What, why, timeline | Team meeting |
| Details | Directly affected | How it impacts them specifically | 1:1 or small group |
| Training | Users | How to use new process/tool | Workshop or doc |
| Go-live | All | Launch announcement, support resources | Email + Notion |
| Follow-up | All | Progress, feedback, adjustments | Team meeting |

### Step 4: Implementation Plan
| Phase | Duration | Activities |
|-------|----------|-----------|
| Prepare | 1-2 weeks | Stakeholder alignment, communication plan, training materials |
| Pilot | 1-2 weeks | Small group tests the change, gathers feedback |
| Roll out | 1-4 weeks | Full deployment with support |
| Stabilize | 2-4 weeks | Monitor adoption, address issues, reinforce |

### Step 5: Support and Training
- Documentation in Notion (how-to guides, FAQs)
- Designated point of contact for questions
- Regular check-ins during the transition period
- Quick wins celebrated to build momentum

### Step 6: Measure Adoption
| Metric | How to Measure |
|--------|---------------|
| Adoption rate | % of people using the new process/tool |
| Proficiency | Quality of output compared to baseline |
| Satisfaction | Survey or feedback |
| Business impact | The original success metric |

## Stack Context
- **Notion**: Change plan, communication materials, and training docs in Notion.
- **Resend**: Change announcement and update emails.
- **Vercel**: If the change involves a product update, preview deployments for stakeholder review.

## Quality Gate
- [ ] Change clearly defined with business rationale
- [ ] All affected stakeholders identified
- [ ] Communication plan executed before the change happens
- [ ] Training and support materials available
- [ ] Adoption measured post-implementation
- [ ] Feedback loop active during transition

## Anti-Patterns
- **Surprise changes** — announcing a change the day it happens guarantees resistance.
- **No "why"** — people resist changes they do not understand. Always explain the reason.
- **Big bang** — rolling out everything at once is risky. Pilot first, then roll out.
- **No support** — expecting people to figure it out on their own guarantees low adoption.
- **Ignoring resistance** — resistance is feedback. Listen to it, address concerns, adjust if needed.
