# Meeting Prep

## When to Use
- Preparing for a sales meeting or demo
- Meeting with a potential partner or investor
- Client kickoff meeting
- Any meeting where preparation improves the outcome

## Workflow

### Step 1: Stakeholder Research
For each attendee, gather:
- **Name and title**: Who are they?
- **LinkedIn profile**: Recent posts, background, interests
- **Company**: What do they do? Recent news? Funding?
- **Relationship**: Have we interacted before? Any shared connections?

### Step 2: Meeting Brief
Create a one-page brief in Notion:
```
# Meeting Brief — [Meeting Name]
**Date**: [Date and time]
**Attendees**: [Names and roles]
**Purpose**: [One sentence — what is the goal of this meeting?]

## Background
[2-3 sentences on the context — why is this meeting happening?]

## Key Stakeholders
- [Name]: [Title]. [1 sentence of relevant background].
- [Name]: [Title]. [1 sentence of relevant background].

## Agenda
1. [Topic] (X minutes)
2. [Topic] (X minutes)
3. [Topic] (X minutes)
4. Q&A / Next steps (X minutes)

## Talking Points
- [Key message 1]: [Supporting detail]
- [Key message 2]: [Supporting detail]
- [Key message 3]: [Supporting detail]

## Questions to Ask
- [Question 1]
- [Question 2]
- [Question 3]

## Potential Objections
- [Objection]: [Prepared response]

## Desired Outcome
[What does success look like? What do we want to walk away with?]
```

### Step 3: Materials Preparation
Gather any materials needed:
- Presentation or deck (if applicable)
- Demo environment (if product demo)
- Case studies relevant to the attendee's industry
- Pricing or proposal documents (if applicable)

### Step 4: Post-Meeting Notes
After the meeting, document:
- Key decisions made
- Action items (with owners and deadlines)
- Follow-up required
- Relationship notes (for future meetings)

## Stack Context
- **Notion**: Meeting briefs, notes, and action items in Notion.
- **Vercel**: If demoing the product, use a Vercel preview deployment.
- **Next.js**: Demo environments prepared on preview URLs.

## Quality Gate
- [ ] All attendees researched
- [ ] Meeting brief created with agenda and talking points
- [ ] Materials prepared and tested
- [ ] Questions and objection responses prepared
- [ ] Desired outcome defined
- [ ] Post-meeting notes documented

## Anti-Patterns
- **No preparation** — showing up unprepared wastes everyone's time.
- **No agenda** — meetings without agendas wander.
- **No desired outcome** — if you do not know what success looks like, you will not recognize it.
- **No follow-up** — decisions without documented action items evaporate.
- **Over-preparing** — a 10-page brief for a 15-minute call is overkill. Scale prep to meeting importance.
